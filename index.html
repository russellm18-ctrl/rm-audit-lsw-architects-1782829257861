// Findings

  • THE STRUCTURAL METADATA COLLAPSE: Your project images—PathHouse, RiverWest, Witherspoon & Sons, Vancouver Clinic—are being indexed as anonymous decorative files with no JSON-LD schema, no structured ProjectType or Architect entity markup, and almost certainly empty or filename-default alt-text. A developer or healthcare administrator in Vancouver, WA searching "mass timber architect Vancouver Washington" is landing on a competitor's structured project page instead of yours, because your 70 years of documented work is invisible to the crawler. Every project image is a dead asset where a geo-tagged, schema-wrapped case study should be sitting.
  • THE HIDDEN TRUST DEFICIT: Public agency procurement officers and MWBE-preference project coordinators—your highest-value repeat clients—are leaving without a conversion trigger because your DBE and MWBE certifications are text claims with zero visual substantiation. No documentation imagery, no process photography showing community engagement, no visual evidence of the actual people and partnerships behind the certification means your claim reads as a checkbox, not a competitive differentiator. That audience is trained to detect performance versus proof, and right now you're only performing.
  • THE COMPETITIVE EXPOSURE: Regional architecture firms competing in the Pacific Northwest affordable housing and higher education sectors are publishing visual case study assets that include construction-phase documentation, material closeups, and occupant-in-use photography—evidence sets that answer the client's actual risk question: "Does this firm deliver?" Your Clark College STEM Building and Vancouver iTech Preparatory projects are credentialed enough to anchor RFQ submissions, but without documented visual proof of process and outcome, a competing firm with inferior work but a complete visual record is winning those shortlist conversations.
  • THE PHOTO-FIRST UPGRADE: Commission a structured visual documentation protocol across your mass timber, adaptive reuse, and affordable housing project typologies—not a single shoot, but a phased asset capture tied to your existing project lifecycle from groundbreaking through occupancy. Every captured asset gets deployed with a strict metadata schema: GPS coordinates embedded in EXIF, project-specific alt-text referencing building type, city, and your firm name, and JSON-LD markup identifying each as an ArchitecturalWork entity with LocalBusiness linkage to your Vancouver, WA and Bigfork, MT offices. This converts your project portfolio from a visual gallery into a geo-indexed, search-structured lead engine that compounds with every completed project.
